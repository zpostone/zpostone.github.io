About This Project

Housing Choice Vouchers are the dominant form of federal housing assistance for families struggling to meet their rent, but only 74% of eligible Pennsylvanians receive one. We’re exploring the spatial distribution of voucher holders across Pennsylvania, what parts of the state have the most unmet need, where might be hit hardest by cuts under Trump’s proposed budget, and how that relates to the Pennsylvanians who turned the battleground state red in 2018.

This project is part of 11.S43 Big Data, Visualization, and Society at MIT. This semester, we are looking at housing and the 2016 presidential election, and how affordable housing policies may be shaped under the new administration.
